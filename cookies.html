Section 1

What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files placed on your device (computer, tablet, or smartphone) by a website when you visit it. They are widely used to make websites work, to work more efficiently, and to provide information to site owners.

Cookies are not programs and cannot carry viruses or malware. They can only be read by the domain that set them (first-party cookies) or, in the case of third-party cookies, by the domain that placed them regardless of which site you are on.

Similar technologies include:

  • Web beacons / pixel tags: Tiny invisible images embedded in pages or emails that register when content is loaded, confirming your IP address, browser, and time of access.
  • Local storage (localStorage / sessionStorage): Browser-side key-value stores that persist data without an expiry (localStorage) or only for the browser session (sessionStorage).
  • Fingerprinting: We do not use device fingerprinting for tracking purposes.
Section 2

Why we use cookies

We use cookies and similar technologies to:

  • Keep you signed in and remember your session across page loads.
  • Remember your language and display preferences.
  • Protect the Platform against CSRF and other security threats.
  • Understand how visitors use our Platform so we can improve it.
  • Measure the effectiveness of features and A/B tests.
  • Send relevant communications where you have opted in to marketing.

We do not use cookies to serve third-party advertising, build advertising profiles, or sell your data to data brokers.

Section 3

Categories of cookies

We classify cookies by purpose. The categories align with the guidance of the Italian Garante and the ePrivacy Directive (2002/58/EC as amended):

3.1 Strictly necessary (Essential)

Required for the Platform to function. They cannot be switched off. They do not require consent under the ePrivacy Directive and GDPR because they are set in response to actions you take (e.g., logging in, setting a language). Examples: session tokens, CSRF protection tokens, load-balancing cookies.

3.2 Functional

Enable enhanced functionality and personalisation. They may be set by us or by third-party providers whose services we have added to our pages. If you do not allow these cookies, some services may not function properly. Examples: language preference, display theme.

3.3 Analytics / Performance

Allow us to count visits and measure traffic sources so we can improve the performance of the Platform. All information collected is aggregated and therefore anonymous. If you disable these cookies we will not know when you have visited. Examples: Matomo analytics.

3.4 Marketing

Set through our Platform by email marketing partners. They may be used to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant messages in our emails. They do not store directly personal information but are based on uniquely identifying your browser. Consent is required. Example: Mailgun tracking pixel in emails.

Section 6

Local storage and similar technologies

In addition to cookies, we use the browser's localStorage API to persist certain preferences and authentication state on your device. Unlike cookies, localStorage data is never automatically sent to our servers with every request — it is read and written exclusively by client-side JavaScript running on the Platform.

The specific localStorage keys we use are listed in the table in Section 4. You can inspect and clear localStorage at any time using your browser's developer tools:

  • Chrome/Edge: DevTools → Application → Local Storage → codicefacile.it → right-click to delete individual keys or "Clear All".
  • Firefox: DevTools → Storage → Local Storage → codicefacile.it.
  • Safari: Web Inspector → Storage → Local Storage → codicefacile.it.

We do not use IndexedDB, Web SQL, or Cache API for storing personal data.

Section 7

Managing your preferences

7.1 Cookie preference centre

When you first visit the Platform, a cookie consent banner allows you to accept all cookies, reject non-essential cookies, or customise your preferences by category. You can change your preferences at any time by clicking "Cookie settings" in the footer of any page.

7.2 Browser settings

You can configure your browser to refuse some or all cookies, or to alert you when websites set or access cookies. Note that disabling strictly necessary cookies will prevent you from logging in or using core Platform features.

Browser-specific instructions

Google Chrome: Settings → Privacy and security → Cookies and other site data → Block third-party cookies (or "See all site data and permissions" to manage per-site).

Mozilla Firefox: Settings → Privacy & Security → Enhanced Tracking Protection → Custom → select cookie blocking level. To clear cookies: Settings → Privacy & Security → Cookies and Site Data → Clear Data.

Apple Safari: Preferences → Privacy → "Prevent cross-site tracking" and "Block all cookies" (note: blocking all cookies may break sign-in).

Microsoft Edge: Settings → Cookies and site permissions → Cookies and site data → Manage and delete cookies.

7.3 Opt-out tools

For analytics cookies specifically, Matomo respects the browser's Do Not Track signal and also provides an opt-out mechanism:

  • Matomo opt-out: Available via the cookie preference centre. Sets a persistent mtm_consent_removed cookie in your browser. Clearing your cookies will reset this preference — return to the preference centre to re-apply.
  • Google opt-out (for GIS): You can manage what data Google collects via myaccount.google.com/data-and-privacy.

7.4 Effect of disabling cookies

Disabling essential cookies will prevent you from logging in, maintaining a session, or using the code-sharing features of the Platform. Disabling functional cookies means your language preference will not be saved between visits. Disabling analytics cookies has no effect on the features you can use; we will simply have less data for product improvements.

Section 8

Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals to websites that you do not want your browsing activity tracked. There is currently no universally accepted standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals.

However, Matomo analytics — the only analytics tool we use — is configured to honour DNT signals. If your browser sends a DNT header, Matomo will not track your visit. No cross-site tracking of any kind is performed by CodiceFacile regardless of DNT status.

We do not use advertising networks or retargeting services, so third-party ad tracking is not applicable to the CodiceFacile Platform.
